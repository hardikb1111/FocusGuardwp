Privacy-first browser extension

FG Focus Guard – Privacy Policy

Focus Guard is designed to protect your attention, not to track you. This document explains in clear terms how the extension handles your data.

Last updated: 10 December 2025
Applies to: Chrome extension “Focus Guard – Block Shorts & Reels”
1. Data collection

We do not collect personal data

Focus Guard runs entirely in your browser. It does not send any information to external servers and does not integrate with any analytics or advertising platforms.

  • No account is required and no authentication data is used.
  • No browsing history is collected, stored, or transmitted.
  • No personal identifiers such as name, email, or IP address are collected.
  • No content from the pages you visit is uploaded or analyzed remotely.

All logic for blocking YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels runs locally inside the extension.

2. Local storage

What is stored on your device

Focus Guard uses the browser’s local storage only to remember your settings and counters:

  • Whether blocking is enabled for YouTube Shorts.
  • Whether blocking is enabled for Instagram Reels.
  • Simple numeric counters for how many times content was blocked.

This information is stored only in your browser. You can reset it at any time from the extension popup or by clearing your browser data.

3. Permissions

Why the extension requests certain permissions

  • "tabs" – used only to detect when the current tab is on a YouTube or Instagram URL so the extension can apply blocking rules. The extension does not read full browsing history or page content.
  • "storage" – used to store your on/off preferences and local block counters. This data never leaves your device.
  • Host permissions for YouTube and Instagram – required to inject a content script that hides or disables Shorts/Reels UI elements on those sites. The script only manipulates layout; it does not scrape or transmit data.
4. Data sharing

No data is shared with third parties

Focus Guard does not share any data with third parties. Specifically:

  • No data is sold, rented, or traded.
  • No data is sent to external APIs or remote databases.
  • No integrations with ad networks or tracking pixels are used.

If this ever changes in a future version, this policy will be updated before any new data use begins.

5. Children & sensitive data

No profiling or sensitive data

The extension does not create user profiles and does not attempt to infer interests, demographics, or other characteristics. It does not process any sensitive categories of data such as financial information, health data, or information about children.

6. Contact

Questions about this policy

If you have questions or concerns about how Focus Guard handles data, you can contact the developer using the support link on the Chrome Web Store listing.

When you contact the developer, any information you choose to provide will be used only for responding to your inquiry.
